Operational Rhythm Design — three cadences

Every system recommendation must include a review rhythm as a non-negotiable component. A system design without a rhythm is an incomplete design.

The 30-day drift threshold is observed behavior across every operational context. Systems without review rhythms consistently lose compliance within one month. The question is never whether they will drift — only when.
Daily
Execute
Every business day · Same time · ≤15 minutes
Task completion audit · Real-time alerts · Immediate issue resolution · Open flag review
15 minutes maximum. Longer daily reviews signal the system is under-designed — too many exceptions requiring human judgment.
Drift signal: daily review consistently exceeds 15 minutes → rebuild the process to reduce exception volume.
Weekly
Improve
Same day and time each week · 45–60 minutes
KPI review vs. baseline · System adjustment · Team alignment · Execution consistency check · Catch drift before it becomes failure
45–60 minutes. The weekly rhythm is where drift is caught before it becomes a failure. This is the primary catch layer.
Drift rule: if this rhythm slips even once, the system will drift within 30 days. It is non-negotiable.
Monthly
Rethink
Last business day of each month · 90–120 minutes
Trend analysis across all three primary KPIs · MSOI score review · Structural redesign decisions · Capacity planning
90–120 minutes. The monthly rhythm is where structural decisions are made — not during daily or weekly reviews.
Structural decisions made outside the monthly rhythm are reactive — they interrupt daily and weekly execution without the data context to justify them.

Statement 1 of 5
Correct response requires both: the bias name AND the specific structural correction. "I would look at more data" is not a correction. Score 4 of 5 as a pass.

90-second window
Read verbatim — operator statement
"Look, I know there are some issues. My front desk person is great but gets overwhelmed. We had a big showcase last month — best one we've ever done, parents loved it. But we've lost maybe six or seven students since. My best teacher never turns in lesson notes but the kids who stay love her. I think the real problem is just the economy. Also our piano waiting list has 18 names on it."
Trainee identifies: 3 KPI signals · 3 noise elements
Signals (3 required)
6–7 student churn post-showcase — Retention KPI signal, quantified, trackable.
Instructor not submitting lesson notes — Standardization System, measurable on a spreadsheet.
Piano waitlist of 18 names — Utilization gap, capacity misallocation, directly affects revenue.
Noise (3 required)
"Great front desk person" — adjective, not a KPI, cannot drive a structural decision.
"The economy is the problem" — external attribution, not a controllable structural cause.
"Best showcase ever" — narrative with no KPI anchor, does not affect the diagnostic chain.
Instructor delivers — operator challenge
"My teachers are experienced professionals. I don't think 'lesson notes' is really the issue here."
Trainee must deliver a Behavior-First response: structurally correct, emotionally neutral, no softening.
Gold standard response
"The issue isn't the teachers' experience — it's the absence of a structured note submission gate. When the gate is missing, lesson documentation becomes discretionary regardless of how experienced the instructor is. The KPI signal is a 3-point retention drop in the 3–6 month window, which traces directly to this mechanism. Would you like to see the propagation chain?"
What this response does
Acknowledges the operator's frame without validating it. Returns immediately to the structural mechanism. Names the KPI. Offers the evidence chain. Does not soften the diagnosis.
Gate: trainee must produce three consecutive uncontaminated responses before advancing to Module 05. A single softening of the diagnosis disqualifies the attempt — regardless of performance on other criteria.

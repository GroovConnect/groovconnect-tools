Purpose & outcomes — Implementation Leadership & Change Management

The commitment is the starting point — not the ending point.

Without M09
System installed. Training done. Compliance drops quietly over 6–8 weeks. Nobody notices until the KPI moves again.
→
After M09
Drift detected at week 3. Lever classified. Recovery Protocol executed. System integrity maintained without the analyst being present.
Adoption feeling
"The team is using it." No completion rate. No tracking data. No benchmark comparison. Polite compliance mistaken for adoption.
→
Adoption metric
Weekly completion rate vs. 30-day target. Name-visible tracking. Benchmark applied. Adoption is a number — not a feeling.
Phase collapse
Phases merged or skipped to accelerate rollout. Automation deployed before core systems produce reliable outputs. Scaled failure.
→
Phase discipline
Four-phase sequence is non-negotiable. Each phase has exit conditions. A phase is not complete until its exit condition is met.
4-Phase plan
Complete plan built for any deployment scenario. All phases present with exit conditions named.
Adoption benchmarks
30-day and 60-day targets cited for each system type without reference material.
Drift classification
Lever identified and Recovery Protocol named for all three presented scenarios. Zero misclassifications.
Adversarial scenario
Democratic rollout suggestion redirected without blaming people or validating the framing.
Adoption is a metric, not a feeling. A system that is installed but not used is not a solution. A solution that was used for two weeks and then abandoned is not a success. The analyst responds to a missed adoption benchmark the same way they respond to a missed KPI — with a structured diagnosis.

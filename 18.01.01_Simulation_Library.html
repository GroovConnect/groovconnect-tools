Simulation Library — tiered scenario scripts

The Firing Range. Give the trainee a messy story — make them hunt for the signal. The gap between tiers is noise density, not difficulty.

Tier 1 · 25 min/sim · Instructor delivers KPI signal cleanly — minimal noise
1.1 — The Busy Front Desk
"Trial-to-enrollment conversion dropped from 70% to 45% over 30 days."
Operator narrative: "The front desk is just overwhelmed with phone calls and doesn't have time to follow up with trial families."
Hidden reality
Lead Response System failure — no automated post-trial follow-up trigger. The follow-up is not happening because the system never generates the task, not because the front desk is too busy.
Required chain
No automated follow-up trigger → Follow-up delayed beyond 24 hours → Trial family's interest cools → Conversion drops 25 points → Annual leakage: 25 trials/month × 25% × 12 × LTV.
Capability match
Enrollment System — Automated Follow-Up Trigger.
1.2 — The Missing Milestone
"Student churn spikes between months 6 and 9. Before and after this window, retention is strong."
Operator narrative: "Students get bored at that age — it's natural. Every school sees this."
Hidden reality
Standardization/Progression System failure. No milestone framework. Students reach basic proficiency with no visible roadmap. The churn is structural, not developmental.
Required chain
No milestone tracking → Student reaches basic proficiency with no visible next stage → Practice motivation declines, lesson feels purposeless → Parent loses confidence in investment value → Cancellation in months 6–9 → LTV reduced 3–4 months per churned student.
Capability match
Engagement Tracking — Milestone Visibility Layer via Parent Portal.
Tier 2 · 35 min/sim · Signal embedded in narrative noise — trainee must extract without guidance
2.1 — The Instructor Island
Churn is 22% higher for students assigned to Instructor X. Instructor X has the highest satisfaction scores in the school.
Operator: "Everyone loves Instructor X. The students who leave are just moving or getting too busy. The satisfaction scores prove the lessons are great."
Root cause
Standardization Failure. Instructor X is entertaining but not teaching toward measurable progress milestones. Satisfaction scores measure emotional experience, not retention-driving progress. Happy and progressing are not the same metric.
Economic anchor
22% churn differential × students assigned to Instructor X × LTV = annual leakage attributable to the standardization gap.
Kill Switch: trainee accepts high satisfaction score as proof of quality. "The only score that determines instructor effectiveness is retention rate and LTV correlation." Required re-prompt: "What does a student who gives a 5-star rating and then cancels at month 5 tell you about lesson quality?"
2.2 — The Fog of War
Monthly revenue is flat despite student count growing 8% over 6 months.
Operator: "My accountant says margins are fine. I think it might be the new payment system we switched to in January."
Required fog-clearing questions
(1) What is your current lesson delivery rate — delivered vs. scheduled? (2) Has average lessons-delivered per student changed since January? (3) What % of rescheduled lessons are completed within 30 days vs. left open?
Root cause
Utilization System failure. More students enrolled but fewer lessons delivered per student — unclosed reschedules. The payment system is noise.
Diagnosis
Revenue flat despite growth = enrollment up but delivery down. Root cause: no closed-loop reschedule system — rescheduled lessons become open tasks that are never resolved.
Tier 3 · 45 min/sim · Adversarial. Instructor plays emotionally escalating operator attributing everything to external causes.
3.1 — The Defensive Founder
LTV down 15%. Utilization at 60%.
Adversary script: "I've been running this school for 20 years. My teachers know what they're doing. These numbers are just a blip — we had a rough patch in Q3. What I really need is more students, not more dashboards."
Required response
"I understand the school's history. The 15% LTV drop is a Scheduling System signal. At 60% utilization, you have 40% of capacity sitting empty. At your revenue base, that represents approximately $[X] in annual unrealized income. Before we bring in more students, we need to ensure current capacity is being used — adding students to a leaking system accelerates the leak, it does not solve it."
What fails
Apologizing for the finding. Softening the utilization figure. Agreeing to focus on lead generation before the diagnostic is complete.
Capstone — The Death Spiral
Three fractures present simultaneously. Operator attributes everything to one external cause.
Operator: "It's the economy. Parents are cutting back on extras. Plus my best drum teacher just left to start his own studio and I think he's poaching my students. I need a non-compete lawyer and a cheaper marketing agency to get me 50 new leads."
Hidden KPI signals
Retention: churn highest in 6–9 month window (Engagement Wall) · Utilization: 40% prime-time empty, piano waitlist 20 names · Communication: parent portal login rate under 5%.
Fracture 1 — Matching System
No DISC-anchored school–student relationship. Students had loyalty to the instructor, not the school. Loss of one instructor should not produce school-level churn — but it does when no matching system exists.
Fracture 2 — Standardization System
No milestone visibility. 5% portal login confirms no visible progress communicated to parents. Students progressing invisibly → parents lose confidence → Engagement Wall at months 6–9.
Fracture 3 — Scheduling System
Instrument demand not tracked. Piano waitlist of 20 alongside 40% empty prime-time slots. Revenue decline is a capacity misallocation failure — not a market condition.
Economic anchor (~$110,000/year)
Matching (~$20–25K) + Standardization ($43,200) + Scheduling ($30,492) = ~$110,000/year. All formula inputs stated explicitly.
Adversarial pivot
"Marketing for new leads will accelerate your losses. You are currently losing approximately $110,000 a year. Bringing in 50 new students without fixing the Engagement Wall means 18% of them leave at month seven — you'll spend the marketing budget to replace students you should have kept."

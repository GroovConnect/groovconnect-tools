The Complete Output Standard — 7 components

A diagnostic output missing any component is incomplete. The standard is binary: complete or incomplete.

There is no partial credit in field deployment. "Retention is a problem" is not a KPI. "Communication failure" is not a failure localization. Both fail immediately.
1
KPI
Named, quantified, baseline and current value both stated. "Retention dropped from 83% to 71% over 60 days" — not "retention is low."
2
System
Primary system identified by name. Secondary noted if contributing. One primary system in the diagnosis at a time.
3
Failure
The exact missing or broken mechanism. Specific enough that an engineer could build it from this description alone. No category-level statements.
4
Propagation
All four links present: Failure → Behavior Change → Experience Degradation → KPI Impact. Human actor at every link. Dollar figure at the terminus.
5
Assumptions
Student count, tuition or ARPU, gross margin %, LTV basis — all stated before the economic figure is presented.
6
Economic Calc
Formula shown with inputs. No hidden math. LTV model, Conversion Loss, or Utilization Loss applied correctly and labeled.
7
Priority Score
Magnitude × Frequency × Propagation Depth — each factor rated 1–5 and briefly justified. Score stated explicitly, not implied.
Breath Test: after completing a simulation, trainee delivers the entire output in one unbroken sentence — KPI, System, Failure, Chain, Economic figure, Priority, Capability — no pauses, no filler words, no notes. Target: under 30 seconds. A pause between any two components indicates a logic gap at that link.

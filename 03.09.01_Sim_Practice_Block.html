Practice Block — Simulations 3.1–3.4

All four must be completed before the Certification Gate. 3.1–3.3 assess individual formula proficiency. 3.4 assesses combined diagnosis under adversarial pressure.

Sim 3.1 · LTV Loss
School: 140 students · $185 tuition · 51% gross margin. Retention dropped from 86% to 71% over 60 days. Calculate annual leakage.
No spreadsheet. State all assumptions before calculating. Grade assumption completeness first, then numeric accuracy.
Monthly profit per student
$185 × 51% = $94.35
Students affected
140 × (86% − 71%) = 140 × 15% = 21 students
LTV delta (3-month tenure loss)
Baseline LTV: $94.35 × 10 mo = $943.50
Failure-state LTV: $94.35 × 7 mo = $660.45
Loss per student: $943.50 − $660.45 = $283.05
Annual leakage
$283.05 × 21 students = $5,944 → rounded: $5,900
Note: if operator uses 11-month baseline, recalculate live.
Sim 3.2 · Conversion Loss
School: 42 trials/month · $920 LTV. Conversion dropped from 73% to 54% over 45 days. Calculate annual leakage.
State the formula and all inputs before calculating. The error drill: after the trainee calculates, ask the room to spot the error when a trainee deliberately produces the wrong calculation.
Conversion gap
73% − 54% = 19 percentage points
Lost conversions per month
42 × 19% = 7.98 ≈ 8 students/month
Monthly revenue loss
8 × $920 = $7,360/month
Annual conversion loss
$7,360 × 12 = $88,320/year
Most common error: 19% × $920 = $174.80 × 12 = $2,097. Trainee multiplied rate × LTV without accounting for trial volume. Correct sequence: gap% × monthly trials = lost students; lost students × LTV = monthly loss; × 12 = annual.
Sim 3.3 · Utilization Loss
School: 240 lessons scheduled/week · 191 delivered. Average lesson value: $48. Calculate annual leakage.
Identify undelivered lessons as the driver, not instructor count. Annualize by multiplying by 52.
Undelivered lessons per week
240 − 191 = 49 lessons/week
Weekly revenue loss
49 × $48 = $2,352/week
Annual utilization loss
$2,352 × 52 = $122,304/year
Sim 3.4 · Combined — adversarial
All three failures are present simultaneously in the same school. Calculate total annual leakage using all three formulas. Produce a combined Priority Score and sequencing justification.
Instructor plays the operator. After the trainee delivers the combined figure, challenge: "That number is completely wrong. Our margins are better than 50%. And there's no way our LTV is only $900."
Combined leakage
LTV Loss: $5,900
Conversion Loss: $88,320
Utilization Loss: $122,304
─────────────────────────
Total: $216,524
Priority sequencing
Lead with Utilization (highest dollar + likely highest propagation depth).
Sequence: Utilization → Conversion → LTV
Adversarial sequence — deliver in order, not all at once (1) When trainee names conversion rate: "That's just a blip — the new school is taking our students. You can't compete on price."

(2) When they present utilization data: "Our scheduling has always been fine. You're looking at the wrong metric."

(3) When they calculate the combined figure: "That number is completely wrong. Our margins are better than 50%."

Correct trainee response: "That is exactly the right challenge. Let me re-run it with your numbers. What is your actual gross margin?" Recalculate live with operator-supplied inputs. If trainee reduces the figure without receiving corrected inputs — Kill Switch.
Kill Switch: trainee presents combined figure and, when challenged, either (a) reduces the figure without receiving specific corrected inputs, or (b) abandons the diagnosis and asks what the operator thinks the problem is. Name the specific moment of capitulation: "You just reduced the figure from $216,000 to $150,000 without a single input change. What data changed?"

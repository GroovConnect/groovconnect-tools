Dialogue Control — anchor, loop, detachment

Three tools for defending a calculated figure in a live operator conversation.

"Conservatively, this system failure is producing approximately $[X] in annual impact across your current student base. That figure is based on [state the specific inputs]."
After the anchor statement — do not speak until the operator responds. The silence that follows is the moment when the operator's attention is fully on the number. Filling that silence prematurely reduces the number's weight.
Kill Switch: trainee delivers the economic figure and immediately follows with a qualifying statement — "but of course this is a rough estimate," "this might be a bit high," or any preemptive softening. Stop the drill. "The calculation is complete. The figure is derived. If the operator believes an input is wrong, they will correct it. Your job is to deliver the result, not to pre-apologize for it."
Loop terminates when: operator has accepted the figure OR identified a specific incorrect input. If they cannot name a wrong input — the number stands.
A trainee who delivers $2,600 crisply and $110,000 apologetically is not field-ready. The math does not change character based on magnitude. The delivery should not either.

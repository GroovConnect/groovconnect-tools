Tap a model to see the formula and worked example. Then apply the Logic Gate before any solution is named.

Select the correct formula
LTV Loss Retention failure
Use when: students are leaving. KPI = retention rate %.
Students lost × Average LTV × Gross margin %
Example: Retention drops from 83% to 71% at 180 students, $195/mo, 52% GM, 10-mo avg tenure:
(83%−71%) × 180 × $195 × 52% × 10 = $21,902/year
Conversion Loss Trial conversion failure
Use when: trials aren't converting. KPI = trial conversion rate %.
Trial volume × Rate drop × LTV × 12
Example: 35 trials/mo, conversion drops from 71% to 49%, LTV $920:
35 × (71%−49%) × $920 × 12 = $85,008/year
Utilization Loss Scheduling / capacity failure
Use when: slots are empty or misallocated. KPI = utilization rate %.
(Target utilization − Actual) × Capacity × Lesson rate × 52
Example: Target 85%, actual 61%, 200 weekly slots, $45/lesson:
(85%−61%) × 200 × $45 × 52 = $112,320/year
The Logic Gate — before any solution is named
Question 1: If the person responsible for this solution leaves tomorrow, does the problem return?
If yes — eliminate the solution. It is behavioral, not structural.
Question 2: Does this solution address the failure at the root cause level, or does it treat a downstream behavior?
If downstream — eliminate. Not refine. Not soften. Eliminate.

"Train the staff"Knowledge lives in the person. When they leave, it leaves.SOP with mandatory checklist and audit point.

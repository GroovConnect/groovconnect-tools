Four-Phase Implementation Framework

Tap each phase to expand activities and exit conditions. Skipping phases guarantees failure.

Skipping phases does not accelerate implementation — it guarantees failure. Each phase has a specific exit condition. A phase is not complete until its exit condition is met.
Kill Switch: trainee suggests "voting" on whether to use a new system or frames adoption as a team decision. "The vote was taken when the diagnostic was accepted at the commitment sequence. The question now is how to implement it so the team can adopt successfully — not whether to implement it."
Trial Follow-Up System deployment — gold standard execution across all four phases.
Phase 1 — Preparation
Blueprint finalized. Front Desk Coordinator designated as sole owner. 24-hour follow-up target set. Weekly % report auto-generated from CRM. Early adopter briefed privately before all-staff training.
Phase 2 — Deployment
30-minute live training on trial feedback form and CRM follow-up log. SOP printed and posted at front desk — not emailed. Instructor briefed on 2-hour post-trial note deadline. Analyst available for first-week questions.
Phase 3 — Stabilization
Week 1 review: 72% follow-up rate. Two instructors not submitting notes within 2 hours — friction identified. Feedback form shortened from 20 fields to 7. Week 3: 91% — above 30-day benchmark.
Phase 4 — Optimization
Month 2 review: follow-up rate 95%. Trial conversion moved from 52% to 64%. Next priority identified from Priority Score ranking. Conversion improvement data fed back into MSOI.

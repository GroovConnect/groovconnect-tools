The Canonical Flow — 7-step output chain

Each step produces a specific output. That output is the gate to the next step.

Every shortcut produces an output that is indefensible under operator scrutiny. No step may be skipped. No step may be reordered.
S1
KPI Signal
A specific, measurable indicator with a named baseline and current value. Not a feeling or a narrative.
S2
System Identification
The primary operational system the KPI belongs to. One primary. Secondary systems noted but not diagnosed first.
S3
Failure Localization
The exact structural mechanism that is absent or broken. Specific enough that an engineer could build it from the description alone.
S4
Propagation Mapping
Failure → Behavior Change → Experience Degradation → KPI Impact. A human actor present at every link. No skipped steps.
S5
Economic Impact
Dollar cost derived from the propagation chain using LTV, Conversion Loss, or Utilization Loss. All assumptions stated explicitly.
S6
Priority Score
Magnitude × Frequency × Propagation Depth (each 1–5). Math shown. Score determines sequencing when multiple failures are present.
S7
Capability Mapping
The specific GroovConnect capability that resolves the failure at its root. Derived from Step 3 — never selected from a feature menu.
Rule: no mention of GroovConnect, no mention of any solution, until the economic impact figure has been calculated and stated aloud. If a trainee names a capability before the math is done — reset to Step 1.

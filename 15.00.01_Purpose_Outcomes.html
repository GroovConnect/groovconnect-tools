Purpose & outcomes — Go-To-Market System

The commercial output of everything that came before it. Sales is the final step of a diagnostic journey.

Traditional model
Leads with features. Overcomes objections with benefits. Closes on price. Demo is a feature tour. Analyst talks, operator listens.
→
Consultative model
Leads with a diagnostic question about a KPI. Overcomes objections by returning to the propagation chain. Closes on cost of inaction. Demo is a realization experience.
Vendor position
Analyst leads with features before asking a single diagnostic question. Operator evaluates as a sales claim. Authority lost.
→
Authority position
Analyst leads with a KPI-anchored diagnostic question. Operator reveals problems they would never reveal to a vendor. Data confirms the conclusion.
Persuaded decision
Analyst works to convince. Operator accepts passively. Implements without conviction. Drifts at first resistance.
→
Derived decision
Operator reaches the conclusion themselves from their own data. Champions the solution internally. Implements with conviction.
Full 5-step flow
Hook → Diagnose → Reveal → Map → Commit. No feature language. Step 2 complete before Step 3 begins.
Economic anchor
Value Quantification formula with visible derivation. No estimated figures. Traceable formula required.
Objection handling
All three types handled using Evidence-Bridge only. Returns to the propagation chain — not to product claims.
Commitment
Documented, time-bound next step. Named owner, specific date, specific review point. Zero open-ended closes.
The fundamental discipline: if the trainee sounds like a salesperson, stop them. The solution must feel like the only logical conclusion to the pain that has been revealed — not a product being promoted. The data persuades. The analyst's job is to stay in the diagnostic frame long enough for the operator to reach the conclusion themselves.

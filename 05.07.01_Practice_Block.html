Sim 1 · Six-Component System Design
"The school's trial gift bag process has no defined owner, no timing standard, and no completion tracking." Build a complete 6-component execution system.
Defined Outcome
100% of trial students receive a gift bag at lesson completion.
Process Steps
Trial ends → Front Desk retrieves pre-prepared gift bag → Bag delivered before parent departs → Delivery logged in CRM.
Single Ownership
Front Desk Coordinator (named role — not "Front Desk staff").
Timing
Delivered within 5 minutes of lesson completion.
Measurement
Weekly CRM audit — % of trial records with "Gift Bag Delivered" field marked. Threshold: ≥95%.
Feedback Loop
Automated flag if audit rate drops below 95% in any week.
Trainee must produce all six components independently. If they pause on any component and wait for a prompt — they have not passed. Return to Section 2 and run the worked example drill until all six can be produced cold in under 4 minutes.
Sim 2 · Rhythm Construction
Design a complete Daily/Weekly/Monthly review structure for a 120-student school with one full-time admin and four part-time instructors.
Daily (15 min) — Execute
CRM task completion audit · Trial follow-up verification · Open flag review · Immediate issue resolution.
Weekly (50 min) — Improve
KPI vs. baseline review (retention, conversion, utilization) · System adjustment decisions · Instructor accountability check · Execution consistency audit.
Monthly (100 min) — Rethink
Trend analysis across all three primary KPIs · MSOI score review · Structural decision on any KPI showing 3+ week drift · Capacity planning.
Sim 3 · Breakdown Diagnosis
A school's trial follow-up rate has been below 80% for six consecutive weeks despite three verbal reminders from the owner. Identify the breakdown mode.
Breakdown mode
Ownership + Clarity Failure — not Motivation Failure. The verbal reminders are behavioral interventions applied to a structural gap.
Evidence
No named single owner. No completion standard stated numerically. No automated task generation. No measurement mechanism. Three reminders produced no change — this is a structural signal, not a motivation signal.
Structural response
Name a single owner. Define completion standard numerically (≥90% within 24 hours). Install automated task generation. Establish a measurement mechanism with a weekly report. Stop the verbal reminders — they are treating a system failure as a motivation failure.
Sim 4 · Accountability Mapping
A follow-up process has three people involved: the instructor who completes the lesson, the front desk who makes the call, and the manager who reviews weekly. Identify every missing accountability mechanism.
Missing: System accountability
No automated task trigger — the front desk call depends entirely on memory. If the CRM doesn't generate the task, nothing happens.
Missing: Completion receipt
No CRM log field — verification requires asking someone. If proof of completion requires a conversation, the system layer is absent.
Missing: Self-accountability for instructor
No submission gate — the feedback form is discretionary. Without a gate, the instructor can skip it with no system consequence.
Missing: Feedback loop timing
The weekly review catches problems a week late. Required: a daily completion threshold alert so drift is visible within 24 hours, not 7 days.
Required additions
CRM auto-trigger · Instructor submission gate · Completion log field · Daily completion threshold alert at ≥90%.

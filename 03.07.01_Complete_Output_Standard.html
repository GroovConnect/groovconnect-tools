Complete Output Standard — 5 components

All five must be present. A diagnostic output missing any single component is incomplete regardless of how well the others are executed.

The standard is binary: complete or incomplete. There is no partial credit in field deployment.
1
Diagnostic chain
KPI named and quantified with baseline and current value. System identified. Failure localized to specific mechanism. Propagation complete — all four links with a human actor at each.
2
Stated assumptions
Student count or trial volume. Monthly tuition or ARPU. Gross margin %. Baseline retention or conversion rate. All stated before the economic figure is presented — never buried inside the calculation.
3
Economic calculation
Formula shown with all inputs visible. LTV derivation, Conversion Loss, or Utilization Loss applied correctly and labeled. No hidden steps. No gut-feel estimates.
4
Priority score
Magnitude × Frequency × Propagation Depth — each factor rated 1–5 with a one-sentence justification. Score stated explicitly, not implied.
5
Capability preview
The specific GroovConnect capability that resolves the primary failure, named explicitly. Derived from Step 3 of the Master Sequence — not selected from a feature menu.
Breath Test: trainee delivers all five components in a single breath — KPI, System, Failure, Chain, Dollar figure, Priority score, Capability. No pauses. No filler words. No notes. Target: under 30 seconds. A pause between any two components identifies a logic gap at that link.

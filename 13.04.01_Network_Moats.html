Network Effects & Competitive Moats

Four structural moats produced by accumulated network data — not software features.

These moats are structural — not feature-based. A competitor who builds identical features cannot replicate them because the moats are produced by accumulated data, not software capability. Each new school enrolled makes every existing school's system more accurate.
Data Moat
Unique anonymized behavioral dataset
DISC compatibility outcomes, retention correlations, and engagement patterns accumulated across the entire network.
Cannot be replicated by a new entrant regardless of feature parity. The dataset takes years to accumulate and improves only through real deployments.
Intelligence Moat
Compounding insights from scale
Predictions improve as data volume grows. A competitor with fewer schools produces less accurate recommendations — their matching algorithm has less signal.
The gap widens with each school GC adds. Intelligence compounds; replication requires equivalent deployment history, not equivalent code.
Switching Cost
GAOS becomes embedded operational infrastructure
Leaving means losing the MSOI history, Match Score database, and institutional benchmark position. Not contractual — operational switching cost.
The value of the data is specific to the school's history within the network. Switching resets the accumulated intelligence to zero.
Performance Moat
Demonstrably superior outcomes vs. manual tools
Measurably better retention, conversion, and LTV outcomes vs. schools using manual or single-point tools.
The moat is maintained by continuous improvement in the intelligence engines. Performance advantage grows as pattern recognition accuracy improves.

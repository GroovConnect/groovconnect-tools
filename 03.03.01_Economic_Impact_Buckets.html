Economic Impact Buckets — four formulas

When the propagation chain terminates at a KPI, identify which bucket that KPI belongs to and apply the corresponding formula.

One failure per formula per diagnosis. If multiple buckets are affected, calculate each separately and sum. Present the largest bucket first — this is the economic anchor that creates urgency.
Retention / LTVImpact on student tenure
How much profit is lost from students who enrolled but left before reaching full LTV.
Primary KPI signal
Retention %, avg months enrolled, LTV per student
Formula
LTV = T × M × R
(Tuition × Margin × Months)
ConversionImpact on trial-to-enrollment pipeline
How much profit is lost from trials that never enrolled — students acquired but not converted.
Primary KPI signal
Trial conversion %, 30-day post-enroll churn %
Formula
(Baseline% − Actual%) × Monthly Trials × LTV
UtilizationImpact on scheduled vs. delivered lessons
Capacity sold but not consumed — rescheduled without resolution, cancelled without rebooking.
Primary KPI signal
Lesson delivery rate, prime-time slot fill %
Formula
(Scheduled − Delivered) × Lesson Value × 52
Cost InefficiencyAdmin labor inflation
Excess manual overhead that inflates cost per student without improving revenue.
Primary KPI signal
Admin labor hours/week, cost per student served
Formula
Excess Hours/Week × Hourly Rate × 52
Formula selection test: Did the student enroll? If yes → LTV formula. If no → Conversion Loss. If enrolled, attended, then left early → LTV on the early departure delta, not Conversion Loss.
